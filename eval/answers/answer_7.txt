On Christmas morning, Ebenezer Scrooge sends a large prize turkey to the Cratchit family as an act of generosity. This gesture is particularly significant as it marks Scrooge’s transformation from a miserly employer to a figure of unexpected kindness. Scrooge enlists the help of a cheerful boy seen from his window to procure and deliver the turkey from the local poulterer’s shop, with the actual delivery carried out by the Poulterer’s Man. The anonymous gift profoundly enhances the Cratchits' humble Christmas celebration, providing them with a lavish meal they could not otherwise afford [Data: Entities (409, 417, 413); Relationships (387, 11)].

This act is emblematic of Scrooge's redemption and newfound generosity, contrasting with his previous neglect of the Cratchit family’s hardships. The delivery of the turkey serves both as a literal improvement of the family’s modest Christmas feast and as a symbolic gesture, strengthening the story's themes of compassion, transformation, and the impact of charitable acts on the lives of the poor [Data: Entities (274, 275); Reports (4)].
