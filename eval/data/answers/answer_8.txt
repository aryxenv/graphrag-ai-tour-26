At the end of Charles Dickens’s A Christmas Carol, Ebenezer Scrooge undergoes a dramatic transformation from a miserly, unfeeling employer to a generous, compassionate benefactor—particularly towards Bob Cratchit, his long-suffering clerk. Scrooge promises to raise Bob’s salary and to assist his struggling family, especially in caring for Tiny Tim. This act is one of the most significant symbols of Scrooge’s redemption and newfound commitment to kindness and social responsibility.

Scrooge’s Promise to Bob Cratchit
After awakening on Christmas morning, filled with the spirit of goodwill and joy, Scrooge greets Bob Cratchit in his office the next day with uncharacteristic warmth and mirth. When Bob, running late and fearing Scrooge’s old wrath, arrives, Scrooge surprises him by not only forgiving the tardiness but also announcing that he will raise Bob’s wages and will “endeavor to assist [his] struggling family.” He further invites Bob to discuss these matters over a warm Christmas fire. This gesture is crucial, as it directly addresses the Cratchit family's financial hardships and signifies a complete reversal of Scrooge’s previous neglectful and stingy treatment [Data: Entities (13, 204, 12, 274, 46); Reports (4)].

Broader Impact on the Cratchit Family
Scrooge’s pledge results in immediate and future benefits for the Cratchit family. It ensures that Bob is better compensated for his loyalty and hard work and that Tiny Tim will receive the medical care and attention needed for his survival and happiness. The story famously concludes with Scrooge becoming “as good a friend, as good a master, and as good a man as the good old city knew,” and he even acts “like a second father” to Tiny Tim, who does not die as predicted in the visions, thanks largely to Scrooge’s intervention and support [Data: Entities (260, 275, 269)].

Significance
Scrooge’s promise marks a turning point, not only in his own life but also in the fortunes of the Cratchit family. It fulfills the message of redemption and the social gospel that Dickens sought to convey, emphasizing the importance of charity, empathy, and personal transformation in the face of social inequality [Data: Reports (4); Entities (13, 18, 260, 275, 269)]. Scrooge’s actions serve as an enduring literary example of the difference individual compassion and responsibility can make in the lives of others.
