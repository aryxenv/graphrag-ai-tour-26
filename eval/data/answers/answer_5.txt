Scrooge's Former Fiancée: Name and Reason for Ending the Engagement
Scrooge's former fiancée is named Belle. She is depicted as a compassionate and insightful woman who was once deeply connected to Scrooge as his sweetheart and later became happily married to someone else, creating a loving family—thus embodying the warmth and happiness that Scrooge ultimately misses in his own life [Data: Entities (24, 197, 205); Relationships (23, 311, 286, 296, +more)].

Reason for the Breakup
Belle ended their engagement because Scrooge's character changed as he grew older: he became increasingly obsessed with wealth and material gain, prioritizing these over love and personal relationships. Belle gently tells Scrooge that "another idol has displaced me... a golden one," signifying that his pursuit of financial security and fear of poverty have eclipsed his affection for her. She notes that his "nobler aspirations" have been replaced by a master passion for gain, making their former contract—based on mutual love and contentment in poverty—irrelevant as he is no longer the same man she agreed to marry. Recognizing this shift, Belle releases Scrooge from their engagement, expressing that his changed nature means he would no longer truly seek or value her [Data: Sources (15, 16); Entities (24, 197, 205, +more); Relationships (286, 297, 298, 296)].

Commentary
The breakup scene underscores a central theme of Dickens’s A Christmas Carol: the tension between material ambition and personal fulfillment. Belle’s heartbreak and selfless release of Scrooge serve as a moral turning point in the story, highlighting how choices shaped by avarice can lead to lifelong regret and emotional isolation—a fate that haunts Scrooge when the Ghost of Christmas Past confronts him with these memories [Data: Sources (15, 16); Relationships (298, 296)].
