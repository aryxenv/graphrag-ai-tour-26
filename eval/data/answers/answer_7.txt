Scrooge's Gift to the Cratchit Family
On Christmas morning, Ebenezer Scrooge sends the Cratchit family a large turkey as a surprise gift. This act marks a turning point in Scrooge's character, illustrating his transformation from a miserly employer to a generous benefactor. The turkey is procured after Scrooge sees a cheerful boy from his window, whom he commissions to purchase and deliver the prize turkey from the poulterer's shop. The delivery of the turkey becomes the first tangible gesture of Scrooge’s new-found generosity and compassion towards others, particularly towards his impoverished clerk, Bob Cratchit, and his family [Data: Entities (409, 413, 417); Relationships (387, 11)].

Significance of the Gift
The delivery of the large turkey is especially meaningful because the Cratchit family, prior to Scrooge's intervention, had only a humble goose for their Christmas dinner—a fact treated with great excitement and reverence within the family due to their limited means. The unexpected arrival of the sumptuous turkey greatly enhances their celebration, symbolizing not just material abundance but also the broader theme of transformative generosity that runs through "A Christmas Carol." This moment encapsulates the story’s message about the importance of charity and goodwill during the holiday season [Data: Entities (274, 277, 254); Relationships (450, 469, 468)].
