Parlour Games at Fred's Christmas Party
At Fred's Christmas party, one of the most notable games played is a lively guessing game that fits the tradition of Victorian parlour entertainment. Specifically, the group enjoys a game where Fred, Scrooge's nephew, thinks of a subject—an animal in this case—and the other guests try to guess what it is by asking a series of yes-or-no questions. The Riddle and Its Answer
During this round of the game, Fred offers a cryptic description of the "animal": it's a live animal, rather disagreeable, savage, sometimes growls and grunts, talks sometimes, lives in London, walks about the streets, and isn't a typical domestic animal or one found in a menagerie. Laughter and playful guesses ensue until the plump sister—an enthusiastic party guest—excitedly exclaims that she knows the answer: "It's your uncle Scro-o-o-o-oge." The answer to the riddle is, indeed, Scrooge himself! Context and Significance
This game and its outcome reinforce the warmth and camaraderie of Fred’s Christmas circle. In bringing everyone together through laughter at Scrooge’s expense (yet ultimately toasting him with goodwill), the game reflects the group’s spirit of inclusion and gentle hope for Scrooge’s transformation.
